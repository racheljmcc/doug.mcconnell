"If it is art, it is not for all. And if it is for all, it is not art."
-Arnold Schoenberg

This provocative statement from Arnold Schoenberg has some truth to share. At the same time, worrying about whether one can create art and what this means is dangerous territory. Kurt Weill once said that he didn't care very much about posterity. He has a good point; have the courage to write what you believe in and accept the consequences from there.

I want to compose music that satisfies my own creative interests: if I don't like what I hear, how can I expect anyone else to do the same? Beyond that, I believe in a model called the composer-as servant, one who writes music for a larger and more general audience. This route still leaves plenty of room for challenge, either in the composing of a given work, or in its presentation, as some of the people who have presented my music will tell you! My thanks to the wonderful teachers, mentors and musicians who have enriched my life and made the works listed on this website a reality.

News

AGO Concert

Joan and I enjoyed the opportunity to share my recent piece, Your Morning Rises as a Song, for saxophone and organ, a piece dedicated to the memory of Dr. Leanne Fazio, at an AGO concert in Toledo this past weekend. The next performance of this work will be in Mississippi, with Joan and Gail Levinsky as the performers.

Joan and I would like to invite all of our Mississippi friends in Starkville, Columbus and elsewhere to a special concert of my music that will take place on Tuesday Feb. 10th at 7:30 PM at First Baptist Church in Starkville, sponsored by the Department of Music at Mississippi State University. Please come if you can; Joan and I would love to see you! Help us to bring Leanne's piece home to her family and friends!

Heidelberg University Guest Artist Program

Thanks to everyone who came to our Guest Artist program at Heidelberg on Friday night, or were with us in spirit from other ports of call! Gail Levinsky and Chi-Chen Wu provided a wonderful program for our local audiences, and I was so very pleased with their amazing performance of my Happy Endings suite. A great night all around! — with Gail Levinsky and Chi-Chen Wu.

Heidelberg University School of Music Present

Joan and I have a big music weekend coming up soon, and you are invited!

On Friday, November 15th, at 7 PM, in the Ohl Concert Hall on the Heidelberg campus, Gail Levinsky, saxophone, and Chi-Chen Wu, piano, will present a Guest Artist recital program at Heidelberg. Among other works, they will present the local premiere of my suite, "Happy Endings," for tenor saxophone and piano. This is a 4-movement composition that is my tribute to the composer Kurt Weill.

On Sunday, Nov. 17th, at 3 PM, Joan will present her annual Faculty Artist recital program at Trinity UCC, 131 E. Market Street in Tiffin. Gail is also on that program, playing two pieces with Joan. One will be the world premiere of 'Your Morning Rises as a Song," a new work for alto saxophone and organ, composed in memory of a mutual dear friend and colleague, Dr. Leanne Fazio. A lot of good friends are coming together to make some fine music! Please join us if you are in the area! — with Gail Levinsky.
